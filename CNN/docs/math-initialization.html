Weight Initialization

Proper weight initialization is critical for training deep networks. Poor initialization can lead to vanishing or exploding gradients, preventing the network from learning.

On this page

  1. He Initialization

He Initialization

He initialization (also known as Kaiming initialization) is designed for layers followed by ReLU activations. Weights are drawn from a normal distribution with zero mean and variance scaled by the fan-in:

\[W \sim \mathcal{N}\left(0, \frac{2}{n_{\text{in}}}\right)\]

For convolutional layers, the fan-in is:

\[n_{\text{in}} = C_{\text{in}} \times K_H \times K_W\]

where \(C_{\text{in}}\) is the number of input channels and \(K_H \times K_W\) is the kernel size.

Why factor of 2?

ReLU zeroes out approximately half of the activations. The factor of 2 compensates for this, keeping the variance of activations stable across layers. Without it, the signal would shrink by half at each layer, leading to vanishing gradients in deep networks.

Code: Worker<T>::initializeConvParams() in CNN_Worker.cpp uses std::normal_distribution<T>(0, std::sqrt(2.0 / fanIn)) to initialise filter weights. Biases are initialised to zero.
