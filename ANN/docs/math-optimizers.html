Optimizers

After backpropagation computes the gradients, the optimizer uses them to update the network's weights and biases. Gradients are accumulated over a batch of samples and averaged before the update.

On this page

  1. SGD
  2. Adam
  3. Configuration

SGD (Stochastic Gradient Descent)

ANN_CoreCPU.cpp — update()

The simplest optimizer — move each parameter in the direction opposite to its gradient, scaled by the learning rate:

\[ w \leftarrow w - \alpha \cdot \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{\partial L_n}{\partial w} \]
\[ b \leftarrow b - \alpha \cdot \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{\partial L_n}{\partial b} \]

Where \(\alpha\) is the learning rate and \(N\) is the batch size. The gradient is averaged over the batch to reduce noise.

Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation)

ANN_CoreCPU.cpp — update()

Adam maintains two exponential moving averages per parameter — the first moment (mean) and second moment (uncentered variance) of the gradient:

\[ g = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^{N} \frac{\partial L_n}{\partial w} \]
\[ m_t = \beta_1 \cdot m_{t-1} + (1 - \beta_1) \cdot g \]
\[ v_t = \beta_2 \cdot v_{t-1} + (1 - \beta_2) \cdot g^2 \]

Bias correction compensates for the zero-initialization of \(m\) and \(v\):

\[ \hat{m}_t = \frac{m_t}{1 - \beta_1^t} \qquad\qquad \hat{v}_t = \frac{v_t}{1 - \beta_2^t} \]

The parameter update:

\[ w \leftarrow w - \alpha \cdot \frac{\hat{m}_t}{\sqrt{\hat{v}_t} + \varepsilon} \]

Adam adapts the learning rate per parameter — parameters with consistently large gradients get smaller effective learning rates, and vice versa. This makes it much less sensitive to the choice of learning rate than SGD.
